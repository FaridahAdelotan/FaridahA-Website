Lawyer

Growing up in two different countries – Nigeria and the US – with completely different cultures, values and, to an extent, behaviors, have opened my mind and broadened my knowledge of people and taught me how to live and act in a diverse community.My values, beliefs and experiences have shaped me into the person that I am today, but what makes me stand out are the things I’ve learned based on those experiences and how open I am to continue learning. I am very friendly, hardworking, and I am always willing to try new things as long as they are safe and helpful to my future self.

My hobbies are singing, reading, and dancing.I moved to the United States from Nigeria three years ago and in Nigeria I went to a school called Juilliard Academy from the seventh to the tenth grade and when I moved here, I went to Academy for Academic Excellence for my eleventh and twelfth grade. While I was in highschool I volunteered with a children's program at my church and I helped teach Sunday school. I was also active in volunteering my time at Academy for Academic excellence. I volunteered over 40 hours on campus to help raise money to support the High Desert Domestic Violence shelter and the Victor Valley Humane Society. I also served as the Secretary and Co- president of the Interact Club at AAE and I was given a medal for having the most volunteering hours, and a medal for good leadership.

I went to the University of California Riverside and I later transferred to University of California Berkeley because they have an amazing pre law program. During my years in University, I worked as an intern in a criminal defense office,I also worked as an intern to a tax lawyer. I also worked as an assistant to the mayor of my home town, Apple Valley. I was also a part of Model UN in my second year of University which gave me the opportunity to go for multiple conferences in different states like San Francisco. I was also a member of the board in ASB which stands for Associated Student Body in my last year of University. I was the president of this club and I had the opportunity to organize events and work with amazing people. I want to become a person that uses the voice to help people, to help people that have been wrongfully convicted of a crime, or people that feel that the government constantly shuts them down when they gain the courage to speak up for themselves.,In everything I do, I strive to maintain my individuality with contributing to the betterment of myself, school, and the community. I believe that we all have a responsibility to make positive change in our areas of influence, and that goes beyond having excellent test scores and GPA. I’ve chosen to be a lifelong learner who will give back to her community and the world.
